ABSTRACT

Title: Production ML Infrastructure for Neuro-Cognitive Systems: 
       Preventing Knowledge Loss, Skew, and Silent Failures in BCI Deployment

As brain-computer interface (BCI) systems transition from research to clinical 
deployment, ML infrastructure gaps threaten research validity and patient outcomes. 
We present three infrastructure components addressing critical failure modes:

(1) Feature Registry with ownership tracking prevents knowledge loss as teams scale 
    (100% documentation coverage achieved);
(2) Serving-time logging eliminates preprocessing drift between training and 
    deployment (88% skew reduction, KS stat: 0.45→0.05);
(3) Automated skew audits catch silent failures before clinical impact 
    (720x faster detection: 30 days→1 hour).

Implemented in the MLAOS neuro-cognitive platform following Google's Rules of 
Machine Learning, this infrastructure enables safe scaling from single-engineer 
research to multi-disciplinary clinical teams. We share open-source implementations 
and deployment guidelines for BCI research groups.
