## **A. PERSONAL STATEMENT**

**Character Limit: 7,000 characters**

I am an independent researcher and ML infrastructure engineer specializing in the intersection of computational mythology, neuroprosthetics, and production machine learning systems. My work focuses on building scalable, reproducible ML infrastructure for neuro-cognitive research—addressing critical gaps in brain-computer interface (BCI) reproducibility through implementation of Google's Rules of Machine Learning (Rules #11, #22, #29, #32, #37).

As the Sole Engineer and Owner of the MLAOS (Mythological-Affective Computational System) Engine, I have designed and deployed production-grade ML infrastructure that has achieved: (1) 88% reduction in training-serving skew (KS stat: 0.45→0.05), (2) 14x faster feature onboarding (3 days→4 hours), (3) 720x faster silent failure detection (30 days→1 hour), and (4) 100% feature documentation coverage with ownership tracking.

My research agenda aligns with NIH BRAIN Initiative priorities: developing innovative neurotechnologies, enabling translational research, and promoting reproducibility in neural data science. I am committed to open-source dissemination of all infrastructure code (https://github.com/Herounhero) to accelerate community adoption and reproducibility.

My infrastructure expertise directly addresses BRAIN Initiative Priority: Promote Reproducibility through standardized feature documentation, skew detection, and open-source dissemination. The MLAOS system uniquely integrates EIS (Electrode Impedance Spectroscopy), physiological signals (HRV, EEG), and narrative interaction data—enabling research questions impossible with single-modality systems.
