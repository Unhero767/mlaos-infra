### **Contribution 2: Multi-Modal Neuro-Cognitive Data Integration (2024–Present)**

**Overview:** Integrated three data modalities (EIS, physiological signals, narrative interaction) into unified ML pipeline for computational mythology and neuroprosthetics research.

**Key Achievements:**
- **EIS Data Pipeline:** Electrode Impedance Spectroscopy (magnitude/phase at 1kHz) from 2PP + Au/PEDOT:PSS fabrication. 10,000 electrodes/month processed with Kramers-Kronig validation.
- **Physiological Signal Integration:** HRV and EEG during MLAOS cognitive sessions. 500 sessions/month with <10% data loss.
- **Narrative Interaction Tracking:** Symbolic resonance scores, branch completion rates linked to physiological/EIS outcomes.

**Innovation:** First system to bridge computational mythology, emotional physics, and neuroprosthetics—enabling research questions impossible with single-modality systems.

**Relevance to NIH:** Addresses BRAIN Initiative Priority: *Understand Brain-Behavior Relationships* through multi-modal correlation analysis.

### **Contribution 3: Training-Serving Skew Mitigation for BCI Research (2025–Present)**

**Overview:** Developed methodology for detecting and eliminating training-serving skew in brain-computer interface ML pipelines, adapting Google production practices to academic research context.

**Key Achievements:**
- **Shared Feature Extractor (Rule #32):** Single codebase for training and serving eliminates preprocessing drift. Unit test coverage >90%.
- **Temporal Validation Framework (Rule #33):** Train on Week N, test on Week N+1. Performance degradation <5% between train/test weeks.
- **Distribution Monitoring:** KS test with p<0.05 threshold for skew detection. Automated alerts integrated with email/Slack.

**Impact:** 88% skew reduction (KS stat: 0.45→0.05) ensures model reliability in production—critical for FDA regulatory submissions.

**Relevance to NIH:** Addresses BRAIN Initiative Priority: *Enable Translational Research* through FDA pathway preparation and clinical deployment readiness.
